Answers to the questions we hear most.

01
Do I need experience to start?
▼
No. The course is built for complete beginners — we start with the very basics of the freight industry. No prior experience required.
02
What level of English do I need?
▼
To start the course, basic English is enough. To work as a dispatcher you'll want an intermediate level (B1–B2). The course includes materials on professional English for dispatchers.
03
Is there a free option?
▼
Yes. The free plan gives you access to the first 3 modules, the simulator, and the knowledge base. That's enough to figure out whether this profession is right for you.
04
How long does the course take?
▼
The full program is 12 modules, 45–55 minutes each. At 1–2 hours a day, you can finish in 3–4 weeks. Access has no time limit.
05
How does the learning work?
▼
Online, at your own pace. Text-based modules with interactive quizzes, a broker negotiation simulator, an AI trainer, real-world case studies, and hands-on assignments.
06
What is the negotiation simulator?
▼
An interactive trainer where you practice real broker calls. The AI plays the broker and you play the dispatcher. You can work through different scenarios: rate negotiation, TONU, detention, and more.
07
Can I work remotely?
▼
Yes, 100%. Dispatching is fully remote. All you need is a computer, a stable internet connection, and a quiet place to take calls. You can work from any country.
08
Do you help with job placement?
▼
Yes. Pro and Premium students get help with their resume, interview prep, and access to a partner job board. We also help you find your first carrier clients.
09
How much does a dispatcher earn?
▼
Beginners: $1,500–$3,000/mo. With 6–12 months of experience: $3,000–$5,000/mo. Experienced dispatchers running several carriers: $5,000–$10,000+/mo. Income depends on how many trucks you run and the rates you get.
10
How fast can I start making money?
▼
Most graduates land their first client within 1–2 months of finishing the course. Your first $1,000–$2,000 is realistic in the very first month once you have 1–2 carriers.
