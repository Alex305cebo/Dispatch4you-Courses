Question Bank and Toolkit
For the teacher — extra materials, questions, techniques
🎯 Comprehension Check Questions
• Explain the dispatcher profession to me as if I'm your friend who knows nothing about trucking
• How does a dispatcher differ from a broker — name 3 specific differences
• If a carrier earns $2,000 per load, how much does the dispatcher get at 8%?
• Why did freight rates rise 25–40% during COVID?
• Name 3 tools a dispatcher needs to work
• Which quarter of the year has the highest rates, and why?
• What is a Load Board and what is it for?
• Can a dispatcher work without a contract with a carrier?
💡 Analogies for Explaining
Dispatcher = Realtor: doesn't own the apartment, but helps sell it and takes a commission

Broker = Staffing agent: takes an order from a company (shipper) and finds someone to fill it (carrier)

Rate Con = Lease agreement: a legally binding document — you can't break it without consequences

DAT = Craigslist for freight: a marketplace where brokers post loads and dispatchers find them

Q4 = Black Friday: peak demand, everyone wants their goods delivered by Christmas
⚠️ Common Student Mistakes
Confusing a dispatcher with a broker — remind them: a broker holds an MC license and works between shipper and carrier. A dispatcher is the carrier's agent, no license needed

Thinking a license is required — it's not. A dispatcher is an independent contractor working under an agreement

Overestimating income expectations — the first month is $300–800, not $5,000

Not understanding RPM — explain: Rate Per Mile = rate / miles. Minimum $1.80/mile for Dry Van

Afraid to call brokers — drill the script until it's automatic in every lesson
🚀 Facts to Motivate the Student
• The US is short 80,000 truck drivers — the dispatcher market keeps growing
• The average US dispatcher earns $45,000–$65,000 a year
• Top dispatchers with 5–10 trucks earn $100,000+ a year
• The job is fully remote — dispatchers work from 50+ countries
• Starting up costs $200–500 (Load Board subscription + phone)
• You can book your first load after just 2–3 weeks of training
• Amazon, Walmart, Target — the biggest shippers, thousands of loads daily
• E-commerce grows 15–20% a year → more loads → higher rates
🎓 Effective Teaching Techniques
The Socratic method: don't give the answer right away — ask leading questions until the student reaches the conclusion themselves

Dream calculator: ask their target income → work out together how many trucks that takes

Role play: you play the broker, the student plays the dispatcher. Practice the call in every lesson

Anchoring: end each lesson with one concrete action the student will take today

Visualization: "Imagine you're driving down the highway counting trucks — each one is a potential client"
